Why every two weeks works

There are 52 weeks in a year, so paying every two weeks means 26 half-payments. Twenty-six halves equal 13 whole payments — one more than the 12 you'd make monthly. That extra payment lands entirely on principal, shrinking the balance that future interest is charged on.

  • 26 biweekly half-payments = 13 monthly payments per year
  • The 13th payment reduces principal directly
  • Lower principal → less interest every month thereafter → faster payoff

Common questions

Do biweekly payments really pay off the loan faster?

Yes. 26 half-payments a year equals 13 full payments instead of 12, and that extra payment goes straight to principal — typically shaving 4–6 years off a 30-year mortgage.

Does it cost more each month?

No. Each payment is exactly half your monthly amount. The acceleration comes purely from the calendar, not a bigger budget.

Can I do it myself without a paid service?

Usually yes — pay one-twelfth extra toward principal monthly, or one extra full payment a year, for the same result. Skip third-party programs that charge fees for it.
